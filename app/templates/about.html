About

As a self-taught developer breaking into tech, I know firsthand how time-consuming it can be to figure out which skills employers actually want. That's why I built this tool - to help job seekers cut through the noise and focus their learning on what's truly in demand in their local market.

After discovering that programming perfectly blends my love for creativity and technology, I dove into self-directed learning through resources like FreeCodeCamp, The Odin Project, and ultimately Harvard's CS50 intro to computer science course. This empowered me to utilize these skills and interests to create an app that actually solves a real problem I had as a job seeker and early-career learner.

I approached learning programming strategically by reverse-engineering job requirements to focus my efforts efficiently. Recognizing the gap between generic career advice and actual local market demands, I built a tool that provides concrete, actionable information. As a self-taught developer, I excel at finding resources and staying motivated to solve real problems.

When planning this project, I chose Python/Flask for its flexibility, Selenium for robust web scraping, Nominatim (OpenStreetMap) for its open-source reverse-geocoding capabilities, Google Charts for clear data visualization, and Bootstrap and vanilla JavaScript to create a clean and responsive interface. This project let me develop skills across front-end visualization and interactivity, to back-end data processing, and API integration.

I approach problems methodically. When building the scraping functionality, I carefully analyzed the site's structure and implemented robust error handling to ensure reliable data extraction. I naturally focus on understanding why solutions work, not just implementing them, which helps me write more maintainable code.
